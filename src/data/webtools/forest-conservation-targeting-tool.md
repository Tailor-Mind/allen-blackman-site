The Forest Conservation Targeting Tool (FCTT) helps policymakers identify geographic areas where forest conservation policies are most likely to achieve the greatest conservation impact. The tool integrates data on deforestation risk, the costs of conservation interventions, and multiple ecosystem services, including carbon storage, biodiversity habitat, and hydrological services.

Users can adjust the relative importance of different ecosystem services and conservation costs to reflect local priorities. The tool also allows users to upload custom spatial data.

The FCTT has been used by The Nature Conservancy for a USAID Mexico REDD project ($30 million) and by the Inter-American Development Bank for forest conservation initiatives in Honduras.
